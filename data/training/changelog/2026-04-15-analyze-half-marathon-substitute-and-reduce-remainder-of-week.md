Wednesday's planned `8 km` aerobic support run became a `21.27 km` half-marathon effort instead, and it also effectively replaced the missed Tuesday `12 km` hill session. The new analysis links the completed note to both Strava and Apple Health, uses the fused provider data, and records that the run was kept controlled overall but still added meaningful ankle load.

Because the week already received a long aerobic stimulus on `2026-04-15`, the remaining plan from Thursday through Sunday was reduced rather than left pretending a normal consolidation week was still intact. Thursday and Friday now become short recovery checks, Saturday defaults toward rest with basketball treated as a skip unless the ankles are clearly settled, and Sunday's planned `15 km` long run is replaced by a capped `10 km` easy run.

The downstream impact is that this week no longer asks for a second long effort or extra court load after a midweek half marathon. That keeps the training source of truth aligned with the actual run and with the immediate tissue-management constraint the run created.
