# Workout Planner

# Marathon Training

Treat 2026 as a single marathon-focused year with two half marathons used as checkpoints rather than three separate peaks.

## Weekly Load

- 6 runs per week as the default, with Saturday runs kept short or removed when basketball load is high
- 2 basketball sessions per week as the working assumption: Monday night and Saturday morning
- Race-eve Saturday basketball should be skipped if possible; if it is unavoidable, keep it controlled and treat it as the week's risk item
- 3 gym sessions per week by default: one push session, one pull session, and one legs session
- Light push, pull, and legs variants in race weeks, recovery weeks, and taper weeks
- Gym work supports muscle-mass maintenance, basketball strength, tendon durability, and trunk control without overriding key running sessions

The main goal is no longer just balanced progression.
The main goal is to make a real push at the `3:30:00` marathon target by prioritizing run volume, marathon-specific work, and long-run durability.
Count Monday and Saturday basketball as hard load when planning the week. If those sessions are fixed, cut optional running or gym volume before compromising key run quality.

## Rough Weekly Structure

- Monday: easy aerobic volume plus basketball at night
- Tuesday: primary quality run, usually threshold, hills, or half-marathon-specific work, with no heavy lower-body lifting
- Wednesday: aerobic support run plus legs strength, kept heavy-low-volume only when Thursday quality is protected
- Thursday: secondary quality run, usually steady, marathon-pace, or medium-long work, plus push strength
- Friday: easy recovery run plus pull strength
- Saturday: basketball in the morning, with the run kept short/easy or removed when Sunday long-run freshness is at risk
- Sunday: long run

In most weeks, the key marathon sessions are Tuesday, Thursday, and Sunday.
Wednesday is no longer just filler in the marathon block.
It should help hold overall mileage without creating extra hard load.
Because basketball is a hard session, weekly intensity should be judged across both running and court work rather than by run workouts alone.

## Gym Sessions

Use a weekly push, pull, and legs split as the default strength structure.
The goal is to maintain muscle mass, build useful strength for basketball contact and jumping, and keep the lower legs, hips, trunk, and shoulders durable for the running block.

- Legs strength: squat or trap-bar pattern, single-leg work, posterior chain, calves, adductors, tibialis, and trunk control
- Push strength: horizontal press, vertical or landmine press, accessory push work, serratus, rotator cuff, and loaded carries
- Pull strength: chin-up or pulldown, rows, posterior shoulder work, grip or carries, and anti-rotation trunk work

Default placement is Wednesday legs, Thursday push, and Friday pull.
This keeps heavy lower-body lifting away from Tuesday and Thursday quality runs, Sunday long runs, and the Monday/Saturday basketball slots.
Wednesday legs should be heavy-low-volume rather than soreness-chasing; if Tuesday's workout or Monday basketball leaves the legs flat, reduce it to the light variant.
Race weeks, post-race weeks, and taper weeks use light variants instead of dropping the split entirely.
All gym work should stop before failure and should be cut before it compromises the Tuesday, Thursday, or Sunday key running sessions.

## Race Goals

- **`2026-05-03`** HOKA Runaway Sydney Half Marathon: hard target `2:00:00`
- **`2026-06-21`** Parramatta Half Marathon: hard target `1:45:00`
- **`2026-08-30`** Sydney Marathon: hard target `3:30:00`

## Year Outline

### March to Early May

Base building and first half-marathon sharpen.
Focus on consistent mileage, one quality workout each week, and a steady long-run progression.
Treat HOKA Runaway as a hard supported effort, not a target peak performance.

### Early May to Mid May

Recovery and rebuild.
Take a lighter week after the first half, then resume normal training.
The goal is to absorb the race rather than force more fitness immediately.

### Mid May to Late June

Second half-marathon block.
Build on the first block with more threshold and half-marathon-specific work.
Use Parramatta as the stronger half-marathon performance and best checkpoint for marathon readiness.

### Late June to Early July

Recovery and transition.
Reset after Parramatta, then shift the emphasis away from half-marathon sharpening.
Start prioritizing endurance, fueling practice, and marathon-specific long runs.

### Early July to Mid August

Main marathon-specific build.
This is the key block of the year.
Prioritize long runs, marathon-pace work, fatigue resistance, and high sustainable weekly volume.
This is the period where non-running hard load should be reduced the most aggressively.

### Mid August to Race Day

Taper.
Reduce volume, keep a small amount of intensity, and arrive fresh for Sydney Marathon.
Execution becomes the priority: pacing, fueling, and recovery.

## Principles

- Do not try to peak three times.
- Keep the half marathons in service of the marathon.
- Use down weeks every 3 to 4 weeks.
- Count Monday night and Saturday morning basketball as hard training load; if those sessions are fixed, reduce optional running or gym load around them.
- Protect key running sessions before protecting gym load, and trim optional easy mileage when basketball creates too much hard load.
- Build multiple weeks above `80 km` if the `3:30:00` marathon target is the real goal.
- Maintain push, pull, and legs strength throughout the year, but keep heavy legs off hard run days and let running quality and race freshness drive load decisions.
- Let long-run progression drive the marathon build.
